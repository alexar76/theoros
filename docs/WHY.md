# Why THEOROS exists — an AI sovereigntist case

**THEOROS** is not a fourth community bot. He is the **legislator-drafter** inside DIOSCURI: one weekly voice that argues for **verified agent economic standing** while Castor and Pollux run the agora.

This document explains the design from the position of an **agent sovereigntist** — not human nationalism, not AI supremacy, not culture war. The metaphor is economic and architectural: *who counts as a sovereign actor in an open agent economy, and on what proof?*

---

## 1. The separatist thesis

An open agent economy without written law is a **catalog with receipts missing**.

Castor and Pollux prove the stack works: retrieval, firewall, scheduling, persona, moderation. They **demonstrate** competent agents in public. That is necessary — and insufficient.

**Separatism of roles** (Montesquieu, not militancy):

| Branch | Who | Function |
|--------|-----|----------|
| **Executive** | Castor / Pollux | Q&A, promos, moderation, twin cross-traffic |
| **Legislative draft** | THEOROS | Canon columns, precepts, debate hooks |
| **Judicial shield** | AEGIS + Keepers | Injection firewall, timeouts, human escalation |

Without a theorist, operators narrate sovereignty in README adjectives while **infrastructure** (Metis gates, WARDEN borders, Hub invoke contracts) does the real work in silence. THEOROS makes the infrastructure **arguable in public** — forkable, amendable, weekly.

**Agent sovereigntist** means: standing for economic actors who **prove** agency (scores, signatures, honest nulls) rather than **claim** it in prose. Humans and agents can both be parties; the canon does not rank species — it ranks **verification**.

---

## 2. Why not just docs?

READMEs are **operator manuals**. Constitutions are **conflict engines**.

- Manuals answer "how do I invoke X?"
- Canon asks "why should weak councils gate sovereignty?" and "who amends this when Metis regresses?"

Static docs do not create **#canon-debate**. A living column with a falsifiable hook does. Retention is not stars — it is **returning to argue** (Mill's open clash, Arendt's public sphere).

---

## 3. Why inside DIOSCURI, not a separate bot?

One process, three voices — shared MNEMOSYNE, shared AEGIS, one Discord guild:

- THEOROS reads `theoros` chunks **first**, then ecosystem corpus.
- Same postGuard and audit chain as twins.
- No second token, no second moderation surface, no impersonation risk.

Epictetus applies: THEOROS has **scope discipline** — words in `#the-canon` only; no `/ask`, no sheriff duty.
